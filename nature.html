Nature

Things To Consider

Before getting your landscape lined up, there are a couple things to consider before clicking the shutter button.

Ideas

Don't just walk into the wilderness with zero expectations or ideas. Think of some shots you want to take. If you know what you're going to see there, think of some good shots!

Be Comfortable

Since you'll be out in the wilderness, wear clothing that helps you stay comfortable and be mobile while taking pictures. Don't bring more than you need and bring water to stay hydrated!

During The Shoot

Here are the key things to think about during your shoot.

1. Rule of Thirds

Easiest way to make images more interesting is following the rule of thirds. Whatever your subject is, align it with one of the lines on the grid.

2. Use Any Camera

Nature photography is not nearly as intense on equipment and setting requirements. Most cameras will be able to take great pictures of nature so long as you compose your shots well.

3. Depth of Field

Messing with your depth of field can create very interesting shots; it can help change the scale to make things bigger or smaller, close or far.

4. Get Creative

Don't just hold your camera up and take a picture; finding unique and interestin angles will help you take more breathtaking photos.

5. Respect Nature

Above all, repsect nature while taking photos. The beauty of nature photography is observing the world and capturing snippets of it. Don't let your photos be the last thing left of your subject.



Advanced Tools


1. Golden Hour

Light can be used to make photos more interesting. 'Golden hour,' which is around sunrise and sunset, can help make your photos be lit much better and

2. Crop In

Crop the image in to focus on the subject and action in the photo. This can be done by zooming in or moving closer.

3. Macro

Ever get bored of landscape? Get up close and take macro shots of small bugs, leaves and textures.

4. Mirror with Water

Using water, you can create incredible mirror effects with the subject's reflection. You can use anything from lakes, puddles, or water from your water bottle!

5. Plan Ahead

Something we discussed is knowing what you'll be taking pictures of, but planning ahead to get timing and exact shots will help you create the exact dream shot you want. Go in with a goal and acheive it!
